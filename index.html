Columbia University is excited to pilot the Public Interest Technology (PIT) Summer Institute, a collaboration between the Computer Science, Architecture, and Journalism departments. In this program, students will brainstorm, create and deploy interdisciplinary projects that work for the public good.

We are currently seeking applicants who are interested in working in a field dedicated to leveraging technology to support public interest organizations and the people they serve. Anyone, regardless of year or field or major, is eligible!

The 3-week program will be held June 6th to June 28th at Columbia for 20 hours each week. Participants will be paid $1200 for their time and effort.

How do we define Public Interest Technology?

Technology, created through interdisciplinary design processes, which serves the public good. This can apply to any kind of technology that advances justice, equity, and inclusion in society.

We take inspiration from the Ford Foundation's perspective on PIT.
Examples of Public Interest Tech

PIT is a broad field which encapsulates many different types of technology - arts tech, civic tech, education tech, and more! Here are some prime examples of PIT in the real world:

  • Citizen: an application which uses user reports to give real-time crime notifications
  • Stenaapp: an application that connects artists to coffeeshops to display and sell their artwork.
  • CUImpact: an application that connects students on Columbia's campus to activist events based on social justice issue
  • coUrbanize: an application that tackles gentrifcation issues by connecting real estate developers and planners with neighbors
What will I do at the PIT Summer Institute?

  • You will collaborate with people from diverse fields, including Journalism, Architecture, Design, Computer Science, Sociology, to research and build technology that works for the good of the people. Projects will be largely determined by the students but ample mentorship and support will be given.
  • You will meet PIT professionals and learn about PIT in industry.
  • You will join and build a tight-knit cohort of individuals devoted to using their respective skills to advance society in meaningful ways.
